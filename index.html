The problems
PROBLEM 01
Scale outran compliance and support

Reached 6M Spanish users by moving faster than ops could absorb. Automation-first AML at scale = ~60K false-positive account lockouts, no Spanish phone line, no human escalation.

Key takeaways
·Automation-first AML scales linearly; trust problems scale exponentially.
·Support gaps beyond a threshold become regulatory blockers.
·Growth-first models build compliance debt faster than revenue metabolises it.

PROBLEM 02
€100M+ in Spain, no Spanish primary-bank product

1,500 employees in Spain, 50 Barcelona ATMs (first in the world). Yet no mortgages, no BNPL, no Social Security processing. Local spend without the complete product stack.

Key takeaways
·Local hiring and ATMs don't replace a primary-bank product stack.
·Lending roadmaps without a regulatory path are vaporware.
·Users stay as secondary accounts while primary lives elsewhere.

PROBLEM 03
The "transparent pricing" gap

Key takeaways
·Transparent-pricing brands die from small betrayals, not big ones.
·Audit qualifications on revenue are a confidence signal regulators read.
·Weekend markups contradict the founding promise. Users remember.

Key takeaways

·Compliance debt compounds: every fix reveals new issues.

Slide 5 / 6 — WiZink
The biggest missteps
monzo

WiZink

one Spanish Supreme Court ruling destroyed the core
Full research ↗
The problems
PROBLEM 01
STS 149/2020 retroactively made the APRs illegal

Värde bought WiZink in 2014 as a PE play on 20-27% APR revolving cards. Five years of profit, then the Supreme Court reinterpreted the 1908 Ley Azcárate. The legacy book became retroactively usurious overnight.

Key takeaways
·Spanish usury law applies BdE's specific category, not generic credit.
·STS 258/2023 fixed the rule: BdE average + 6pp = usurious.
·Retroactive rulings can void entire legacy books, not just new business.
Verbatims & news
STS 149/2020: usura en tarjetas revolving · Legal Today↗ STS 258/2023: BdE + 6pp rule · Abogacía Española↗
PROBLEM 02
PE roll-up with no Spanish brand to defend them

Assembled from Banco Popular, Citibank Spain and Barclaycard Spain portfolios. 1.6M customers acquired through airport stands and mall kiosks. When the ruling hit, nobody defended them publicly.

Key takeaways
·Yield-extraction models with no brand have no cultural moat.
·Arriaga, Reclamador, Indemniza industrialised "reclamar WiZink" in months.
·Brand equity is insurance against legal shocks you didn't see coming.
Verbatims & news
"Reclamar WiZink" industrialised · Arriaga Asociados↗ "Incapaces de salir de pérdidas en España" · TheObjective 2025↗
PROBLEM 03
Three CEOs, profitability target pushed to 2027

Losses of €97.9M (2022), €49.9M (2023), €22.5M (2024), widening in 2025. €81M revolving provisions in 2024 alone. BdE €2.7M fine Dec 2023 for documentation breaches. 3 CEOs rotated.

Key takeaways
·Provisions for legal tail can outlast the pricing fix by years.
·Crisis-era CEO rotation signals structural uncertainty to the market.
·"Pushed to 2027" means the shareholder clock is running out.
