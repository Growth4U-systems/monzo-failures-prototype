Revolut

won Spain, but on an infrastructure that can't serve it
Who they are in Spain

6M Spanish customers (13% penetration), the 4th-largest bank in Spain by customer count, ahead of ING and Sabadell. Spain is now Revolut's third-largest market after the UK and France.

Strategic thesis for Spain

Win Spain on FX + speed + scale, then hand users a primary bank with local IBAN, Bizum, ATMs and lending. Part 1 worked. Part 2 is where the operating model starts to break.

What broke in Spain
'Primary bank' brand, automated-freeze infrastructure
Compliance runs on automated risk flags. At 6M Spanish users, a 1% false-positive rate = 60,000 people locked out, with no Spanish phone line and no human escalation. Architectural, not fixable: they chose growth over ops.
Massive local investment, still can't do the basics
1,500 employees in Spain. 50 Spanish ATMs (first in the world). Yet: can't process Social Security, no BNPL in Spain (live in IE/PL), mortgages announced for "2026-2027" with no regulatory pathway. Spanish lending roadmap is vaporware.
Trust gap widening, not closing
HelpMyCash 8.4/10 overall but 2.9/5 for Spanish support. Still charges 1% weekend FX markup in 2026. Small thing, big signal: they always extract margin where they can, even at the cost of the "transparent pricing" brand.

Slide 2 / 6 — Bunq · Spain focus
Failures in Spain
monzo

Bunq

seven years in Spain, still trapped in the expat niche
Who they are in Spain

Live in Spain since 2021. Launched Bizum in May 2025 (4 years late), gated behind paid tiers. Tripled Spanish users in 2024, off a tiny base. Treated as "successful niche player", not primary-bank contender.

Strategic thesis for Spain

Win the expat wedge with frictionless no-NIE onboarding, then convert that beachhead into mainstream Spanish primary banking. The wedge worked. The conversion never happened.

What broke in Spain
Won the expat niche so completely they're trapped inside it
The no-NIE onboarding was surgical. But Spanish autónomos need tools Bunq doesn't have (SEPE, AEAT, TGSS, mature Bizum). Scaling beyond expats requires lending and SME products that directly contradict Niknam's anti-debt philosophy.
Support is a Dutch phone number in English
Google Play 3.4/5. No Spanish support line, English-preferring staff, emergencies only. Spanish customers are calling a foreign company, because it is one. Fixing this needs 3-5x headcount, which kills the lean unit economics that make them profitable.
Subscription model fights Spain's Bizum-for-free culture
Bunq gates Bizum access behind €3.99-€18.99 paid tiers. In a market where Bizum comes baked in for free with every incumbent (28M users, 95% coverage), paying to use it is almost culturally offensive. Dead on arrival for native Spaniards.

Slide 3 / 6 — Qonto · Spain focus
Failures in Spain
monzo

Qonto

invested €100M in Barcelona with no product to sell
Who they are in Spain

French B2B neobank, €100M poured into a Barcelona support hub. Spain user count? Never publicly disclosed — which tells you what you need to know. Only 12,100 monthly branded searches for "Qonto" in Spain.

Strategic thesis for Spain

Replicate the France and Germany B2B playbook, use the Barcelona hub as a local beachhead, charge a premium subscription to Spanish autónomos. Ran into Spanish banking culture head-on and bounced off.

What broke in Spain
No free plan in a free-banking market
Qonto starts at €29/month. Only competitor in Spain charging from day one. BBVA, Santander and Sabadell now offer free business accounts with up to €720 in cash incentives. Qonto can't match without destroying the subscription model. Pricing squeeze, no exit.
Cannot be the primary bank for any Spanish autónomo
Not a TGSS collaborator — can't process Social Security payments. No Bizum — missing 30.6M users and >95% market coverage. Every freelancer using Qonto needs a parallel Spanish bank. That parallel account keeps the door open for every competitor.
€100M in Barcelona, no complete product to sell
Research explicitly labels Spain as "limited expansion" vs France and Germany. The product gaps (no Bizum, no TGSS, no free plan) meant the marketing never had a complete offer for Spanish freelancers. Awareness among autónomos remains low.

Slide 4 / 6 — N26 · Spain focus
Failures in Spain
monzo

N26

had every Spanish advantage and still lost to Revolut
Who they are in Spain

~1M Spanish users. Longest-tenured neobank in Spain (entered 2016). First with Spanish IBAN (2019). First with Bizum. Only neobank with Social Security integration. Every structural advantage, and still marginal.

Strategic thesis for Spain

Own Spain by being the first neobank with the full localization stack and the strongest expat-meets-autónomo brand. They built the rails. They never built the brand presence to match.

What broke in Spain
The product was ready. The marketing never showed up
Every structural advantage in Spain. And Revolut arrived years later with none of them and captured 6M users to N26's ~1M. Shallow acquisition, very few salary domiciliations, very few deep users. They had the rails, they never drove the traffic.
2020 SEPE scandal became the Spanish press template
COVID 2020: users who'd closed BBVA accounts couldn't receive unemployment benefits because N26 wasn't a SEPE collaborator. elDiario.es, Cinco Días and El Independiente ran the story for weeks. "Neobank isn't a real bank" became the press template, and N26 inherits it still today.
Institutional fragility makes Spanish users nervous
Both co-founders forced out within 4 months (Stalf Aug 2025, Tayenthal Dec 2025). €80M in compliance, and BaFin keeps finding new problems (mortgage ban in NL Dec 2025). For Spanish customers already at 16% bank-trust baseline, "counterparty risk" is a deal-breaker.

Slide 5 / 6 — WiZink · Spain focus
Failures in Spain
monzo

WiZink

one Spanish Supreme Court ruling destroyed the core
Who they are in Spain

Spanish credit-card specialist, not a neobank. PE-owned by Värde Partners since 2014. 1.6M Spanish customers, ~€3.4B card book. Assembled from Banco Popular, Citibank Spain and Barclaycard Spain for yield extraction.

Strategic thesis for Spain

Carved-out specialist bank in Spain, run digital-only on 20-27% APR revolving cards, exit via IPO or trade sale. Five years of profit, then one Spanish Supreme Court ruling retroactively made the core product illegal.

What broke in Spain
STS 149/2020 blew up the entire business model
March 2020: the Spanish Supreme Court reinterpreted the 1908 Ley Azcárate. 26.82% APR declared usurious. Contracts voided. Excess interest refundable. STS 258/2023 fixed the rule: BdE average + 6pp. Went from €3B revolving platform at ~20% yields to -€52.5M in 2025.
A PE roll-up of card portfolios, not a Spanish brand
Customers acquired through airport stands and mall kiosks. No brand equity, no community, no loyalty. When the usury ruling hit, nobody defended them. Arriaga, Reclamador and Indemniza industrialised "reclamar WiZink" into a household phrase in Spain.
Squeezed middle with no path out in Spain
Spanish universal banks have cheaper funding. Spanish specialists (Cofidis, Cetelem) have BNPL expertise. Spanish neobanks have UX. WiZink's pivot to auto lending + BNPL + personal loans puts them in direct competition with Spanish players better positioned in every vertical.

Slide 6 / 6 — Trade Republic · Spain focus
Failures in Spain
monzo

Trade Republic

1M Spanish users, but none of them see it as a bank
Who they are in Spain

Live in Spain since October 2021. Spanish IBAN and Bizum only from June 2025 (4 years on a German central stack). 1M+ Spanish users by January 2026. Spain is now a top-3 market by volume.

Strategic thesis for Spain

Hook Spanish users with the 4% account, then rewire the brand from "investing app" to "primary bank" before competitors close the rate gap. The hook landed. The rewiring is fighting physics.

What broke in Spain
Biggest revenue stream about to be banned in Spain too
Over a third of income comes from Payment for Order Flow. EU ban hits 30 June 2026. The MTF licence (Jan 2026) is a mitigation bet, not a proven replacement. Either raise commissions (breaking €1 trades that acquired users) or absorb a 30%+ revenue hit.
Mercenary Spanish customers with zero switching cost
Spanish users came for the deal: €1 trades, 2% TAE, free savings plans. No brand loyalty, no local support story. Revolut, N26 and Wise are already matching. When someone beats the offer, the €100/mo Sparplan crowd moves in 5 minutes. Optimised for acquisition, not retention.
Spanish NPS bifurcation: good broker, bad bank
Spain ran on the German central stack with no local ops until June 2025. Phasen research: Spanish primary-bank NPS 10-20 vs 35-45 for investing. Rankia forum: "bueno para tener cash, pero no lo uses como banco principal". The brand rewiring is fighting itself.
